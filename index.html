When You Should Change Your Locks

If your locks are not working smoothly or you're unsure who has keys, replacing them can restore full peace of mind.

Old or Damaged Locks

Over time, locks wear down from daily use and weather exposure. Replacing worn locks prevents future lockouts and improves reliability.

Common Signs You Need Replacement:

  • Key hard to turn
  • Lock feels loose
  • Rust or corrosion
  • Keys getting stuck
  • Door not locking smoothly

Rekey Service — When It Matters Most

Common Rekey Scenarios

If there is any doubt about who may have access, rekeying is the safest decision.

🏠 Just Moved In?

Even if you received all the keys from the previous owner, there's no way to know how many copies were made. Contractors, cleaners, neighbors, or former tenants may still have keys. Rekey immediately for full control from day one.

🔑 Lost or Stolen Keys

If your keys are missing — dropped, stolen from a purse, or taken during a vehicle theft — time matters. Rekeying prevents unwanted access. We provide fast mobile service to secure your property quickly.

👥 Tenant Turnover

After a tenant moves out, rekeying ensures previous occupants no longer have access. It's the fastest and most affordable way to reset access between renters.

🛡️ After a Break-In

After a break-in or attempted entry, rekeying all locks is an essential step in restoring security. We can also evaluate whether a full lock upgrade is the better option for your situation.
